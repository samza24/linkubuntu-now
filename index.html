The problem

Every day in South Africa, people die or are critically injured in road accidents. Their families wait hours, days — sometimes years — to find out what happened. Bodies are buried as paupers because no one could identify them.
The current system fails because it relies on one outdated phone number. When that number is dead, the family connection is completely broken.

How LinkUbuntu works

1

Citizens build their emergency web

Register with your SA ID. Add the phone numbers of people who must be called if something happens to you. Update anytime.

2

Responder scans at the scene

A SAPS officer, paramedic, or traffic officer scans the victim's fingerprint. The system matches against the database and returns their profile.

3

Family notified instantly

Real SMS sent to all emergency contacts in priority order. Responder can also call directly from the app. Family knows within minutes — not days.
